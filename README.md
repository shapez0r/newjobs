# Career Study Plans for Entry-Level Technical Roles

This repository contains certification-style study plans for three job paths:

1. [Data Center Engineer / Data Center Technician](./dc_engineer_or_technician/STUDY_PLAN.md)
2. [DevOps Engineer](./devops/STUDY_PLAN.md)
3. [Site Reliability Engineer, SRE](./sre/STUDY_PLAN.md)

Each plan is written for a **newbie starting from zero** and is designed to reach **one grade above entry level**. The goal is not only to understand the job description, but to become interview-ready for a competitive market such as **Dublin / Ireland**, where even junior candidates are often expected to show practical experience, portfolio evidence, communication skills, and strong fundamentals.

## How to use these plans

- Treat each plan like a private certification program.
- Study **5-6 days per week** for at least **2-4 hours per day**.
- Keep all notes, diagrams, labs, scripts, incident writeups, and screenshots in a portfolio.
- At the end of every week, perform the checkpoint assessment.
- At the end of the plan, prepare a CV, LinkedIn profile, GitHub portfolio, and mock interview answers based on the completed work.

## Recommended order if she is completely new

If she has no technical background, the most beginner-friendly order is:

1. **Data Center Technician / Engineer** - strongest physical infrastructure path and the best introduction to networking, hardware, cabling, Linux, ticketing, and operational discipline.
2. **DevOps Engineer** - adds cloud, automation, CI/CD, containers, Infrastructure as Code, and scripting.
3. **SRE** - builds on DevOps plus reliability engineering, observability, incident response, SLIs/SLOs, and production operations.

She does **not** need to complete all three before applying. She should pick the role that fits her interests and complete that plan deeply.

## Common weekly routine for all plans

| Activity | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| Theory study | 5 days/week | Build vocabulary and fundamentals |
| Hands-on labs | 4-5 days/week | Convert knowledge into proof of skill |
| Notes and flashcards | Daily | Prepare for interviews and recall |
| Portfolio update | Weekly | Produce evidence for recruiters |
| Mock interview | Weekly from week 4 | Build confidence and clear explanations |
| CV / LinkedIn refinement | Weekly from week 5 | Match Dublin job market expectations |

## Job-market expectations in Dublin

For entry-level technical jobs in Dublin, she should aim to show:

- Clear communication in English.
- Strong reliability, punctuality, and willingness to work shifts/on-call if the role requires it.
- Basic but practical Linux, networking, cloud, and troubleshooting knowledge.
- Evidence of hands-on practice, not only certificates.
- A portfolio with diagrams, lab notes, GitHub repositories, incident reports, and screenshots.
- Familiarity with ticketing, documentation, change management, escalation, and customer/stakeholder communication.
- Interview stories using the **STAR** format: Situation, Task, Action, Result.

## Portfolio structure

Create a separate folder or GitHub repository for her evidence:

```text
portfolio/
  README.md
  cv/
  linkedin-profile-notes.md
  networking/
  linux/
  cloud/
  automation/
  monitoring/
  incidents/
  diagrams/
  interview-answers/
```

Every completed lab should include:

- Goal
- Tools used
- Architecture diagram or photo/screenshot
- Steps performed
- Problems encountered
- Troubleshooting method
- Final result
- What she would improve in a real company environment

## Final success criteria

She is ready to apply when she can:

- Explain all major concepts in the selected study plan without reading notes.
- Complete the final capstone project and present it in 10 minutes.
- Answer beginner and junior-plus interview questions confidently.
- Show at least 5-8 practical portfolio artifacts for the chosen role.
- Tailor her CV to job descriptions from Dublin-based employers.
- Discuss reliability, security, documentation, and operational best practices.
